Amagwinya

Amagwinya is a popular South African street food, also known as vetkoek. It's essentially deep-fried dough that's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. It can be served plain or filled with various sweet or savory fillings, making it a versatile and delicious snack.

ingredients

  • flour
  • sugar
  • salt
  • baking powder
  • warm water
  • Cooking oil for frying

steps

  • Mix the Dry Ingredients:

    In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, and baking powder.

  • Add Water:

    Gradually add the warm water to the dry ingredients while stirring. Continue adding water until you have a soft, elastic dough. You may not need the full cup of water, so add it slowly.

  • Knead the Dough:

    Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. This helps develop gluten and gives the amagwinya a nice texture.

  • Rest the Dough:

    Place the dough back in the mixing bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rest for about 30 minutes. This allows it to rise slightly.

  • Divide and Shape:

    After resting, divide the dough into equal-sized portions, depending on how large you want your amagwinya to be. Roll each portion into a ball.

  • Heat the Oil:

    In a deep pot or frying pan, heat enough cooking oil to submerge the dough balls. Heat it to around 350-375°F (175-190°C).

  • Fry the Dough:

    ICarefully place the dough balls into the hot oil, a few at a time, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until they are golden brown on both sides, turning them occasionally. This should take about 2-3 minutes per side

  • Drain and Serve:

    Remove the amagwinya from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  • Fill or Serve:

    f you want sweet amagwinya, you can dust them with powdered sugar or drizzle with syrup or apricot jam. For savory amagwinya, allow them to cool slightly, then slice them open and fill them with your cooked minced meat.

  • Enjoy:

    Serve your amagwinya while they are still warm and crispy.
