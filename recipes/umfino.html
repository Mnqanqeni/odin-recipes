Umfino

Umfino" is traditional South African dish, specifically associated with the Xhosa people. It is a type of African spinach or leafy green vegetable dish that is often served as a side dish. Umfino is known for its simplicity and use of locally available ingredients.

ingredients

  • Dark leafy greens (collard greens, Swiss chard, amaranth, etc.)
  • Maize meal (cornmeal)
  • Onion (optional)
  • Cooking oil or butter (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

steps

  • Prepare the Greens:

    Wash the dark leafy greens thoroughly to remove any dirt or grit. Chop the greens into small pieces. You can remove any tough stems if they are present.

  • Boil or Steam the Greens:

    In a large pot, add enough water to cover the greens, and bring it to a boil. Add the chopped greens to the boiling water. Boil or steam the greens until they become tender. This typically takes around 10-15 minutes, but the exact time may vary depending on the type of greens you're using. You want them to be soft but not overcooked.

  • Prepare the Maize Meal:

    While the greens are cooking, prepare the maize meal. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Gradually add maize meal to the boiling water, stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming. Reduce the heat and simmer the maize meal, stirring regularly, until it thickens and reaches a porridge-like consistency. This may take about 20-30 minutes. Add more water if needed.

  • Combine Greens and Maize Meal:

    Once the greens are tender and the maize meal is ready, you can combine them. Drain any excess water from the greens and add them to the maize meal pot. Mix the greens and maize meal together thoroughly. You can add some cooking oil or butter for extra flavor at this stage if desired.

  • hSeason to Taste:

    Season the umfino with salt and pepper to taste. If you'd like, you can also sauté some chopped onions and tomatoes in a bit of oil until they are soft and add them to the umfino for added flavor.

  • Serve:

      Umfino can be served hot as a side dish alongside your choice of South African stews, meats, or other dishes. It's a versatile dish that complements a variety of main courses.
